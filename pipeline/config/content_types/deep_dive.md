# Deep Dive — Content Type Specification

## Identity
- **Funnel position:** Middle of Funnel (evaluation, trust-building)
- **Schema type:** Article
- **Word count:** 2,000-3,500 words
- **Reading time:** 10-15 minutes
- **Publishing frequency:** 3x/week (Monday, Wednesday, Friday afternoons)
- **Purpose:** Comprehensive analysis that establishes thought leadership. The "read this on the flight" piece.

## Target Personas
- **Primary:** The Strategist (CEO, COO evaluating strategic direction)
- **Secondary:** The People Lead (CHRO assessing workforce impact)
- **Tertiary:** The Compliance Lead (CRO understanding regulatory landscape)

## H1 Format
Declarative claim or contrarian thesis. NOT a question.

Examples:
- "Enterprise AI ROI Is Real, But Only for Companies That Fix These 4 Gaps First"
- "The 78% AI Adoption Stat Is Misleading: What the Data Actually Shows"
- "Why Most AI Governance Frameworks Fail Before They Launch"

Anti-patterns:
- "A Deep Dive into AI" (no thesis)
- "Understanding AI ROI" (passive, no claim)
- "The State of AI in 2026" (report title, not an article)

## Mandatory Sections (in order)

### 1. Lede
Thesis statement + strongest supporting data point. The reader should know your conclusion from sentence 1.

### 2. What the Research Shows
Specific findings in plain language. Name the study, the institution, the sample size, the timeframe. Include the methodology's limitations.

### 3. What This Doesn't Prove (CRITICAL)
This section builds credibility. 3-5 explicit non-claims. "This study does NOT prove that..." Forces intellectual honesty and differentiates from marketing content.

### 4. Implications by Function
Cover 3+ persona perspectives:
- **For Operations Leaders:** [specific operational impact]
- **For HR/People Teams:** [workforce implication]
- **For Finance:** [budget/ROI implication]
- **For Compliance:** [regulatory implication]

### 5. Where This Breaks in Practice
3-5 real-world friction scenarios. Named examples where the theory failed. This is where credibility is built, acknowledging failure patterns.

### 6. Clear Judgment
Explicit, not hedged. State WHEN this applies, WHEN it doesn't, and WHY you're confident.

## Visual Requirements
- **Stat-boxes:** Minimum 2
- **Chart or comparison table:** At least 1 data visualization
- **Infographic:** Recommended (summary visual combining key data points)
- **Cover image:** Generated SVG, deep_dive template

## Internal Links
- Minimum 2 links to related content (How-To, Case Study, or News Analysis)
